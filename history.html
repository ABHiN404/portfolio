HISTORY

1978

The 1978 FIFA World Cup was held in Argentina from 1 June to 25 June 1978. Argentina hosted the tournament for the first time and won their first FIFA World Cup title. The competition featured 16 teams and 38 matches. In the final, Argentina defeated the Netherlands 3–1 after extra time at the Estadio Monumental in Buenos Aires. Mario Kempes was the tournament's top scorer with 6 goals and was named the best player of the tournament. Brazil finished third, while Peru finished fourth. The 1978 World Cup is remembered as a historic milestone in Argentine football history.

1986

The 1986 FIFA World Cup was held in Mexico from 31 May to 29 June 1986. It was the 13th edition of the FIFA World Cup and featured 24 teams. Argentina won their second World Cup title by defeating West Germany 3–2 in the final. Diego Maradona captained Argentina and produced one of the greatest individual performances in World Cup history, scoring the famous 'Hand of God' goal and the 'Goal of the Century' against England. Gary Lineker finished as the tournament's top scorer with 6 goals. The 1986 World Cup is widely regarded as one of the greatest tournaments in football history.

2022

The 2022 FIFA World Cup was held in Qatar from 20 November to 18 December 2022. It was the 22nd edition of the FIFA World Cup and featured 32 teams. Argentina won their third World Cup title by defeating France in a dramatic final that ended 3–3 after extra time, with Argentina winning 4–2 on penalties. Lionel Messi captained Argentina and won the Golden Ball as the tournament's best player, while Kylian Mbappé won the Golden Boot with 8 goals. The tournament is widely regarded as one of the greatest FIFA World Cups ever played.
